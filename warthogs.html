WARTHOGS

There is no denying that warthogs are not the most aesthetically pleasing of Africa’s wildlife. Large, flat heads covered in ‘warts’, four sharp tusks, sparse hair – they are not likely to win any beauty contests

They do have some odd habits – entering burrows backwards, trotting with erect tails, and bending their forelegs to graze but their undeniable charisma will leave a memorable mark on your safari.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

Warthogs live in Africa's southern Sudan and southwestern Ethiopia, in savanna woodland and grasslands
